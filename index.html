Two lanes, one recommendation

Why we think both should move at the same time

There are two separate efforts on the table right now, and the instinct might be to tackle them one at a time. Here's why we don't think that's the right call.

Lane 1

Marketing & assessment visibility

You, Alex, and Sam — this conversation

Connecting the taker → buyer → repeat-buyer path so marketing can see it and act on it.

Lane 2

TGC Opportunity cleanup

Allen and Ben, running separately

Fixing the data structure that ties a contact back to the opportunity they came from.

Here's the catch: Lane 2 is part of what makes Lane 1 possible. The cleaner that opportunity-to-contact structure gets, the more of the taker-to-buyer path marketing can actually see. Wait for Lane 2 to finish before starting Lane 1, and you delay the thing you actually care about. Run Lane 1 on its own without Lane 2, and it eventually hits a ceiling. Doing both at once means marketing starts showing results while the cleanup happens underneath it — and the cleanup gets a real reason pulling it forward, instead of being cleanup for its own sake.

Where it breaks today

The path from taker to buyer, as it actually works right now

Someone takes an assessment. If they bought it themselves, Salesforce has the full picture. But most of the time, someone else bought it for them — and you don't find out who actually took it until after the fact. That information lands in ActiveCampaign or your in-house WordPress tracking. It never makes it back to the Salesforce opportunity that paid for it.

The unlock

Marketing and sales, looking at the same record

Right now, sales owns the opportunity and marketing owns the taker — two separate views of the same customer. Connecting them is what makes everything else here possible.

01

One record, two teams

The same account and opportunity sales is already tracking gets the assessment-taker activity added to it — so marketing and sales are looking at the same information, instead of two separate exports that never line up.

02

Follow-up that runs itself

Segment by taker, first-time buyer, repeat buyer, or certified consultant, and let the follow-up go out automatically instead of rebuilding those lists every time — no extra hands needed on a small team.

03

Know what's actually working

Tie a specific campaign, ad, or email back to an assessment completion or a repeat purchase — so marketing can show sales, in the same system, which efforts are actually contributing to pipeline.

This is Lane 1 from above. Lane 2 — the TGC Opportunity cleanup — is what makes the "one record, two teams" piece fully possible.
